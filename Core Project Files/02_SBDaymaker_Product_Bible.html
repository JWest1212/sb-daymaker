01 — The Essence

What SB Daymaker is, in one breath

A mobile-first daily companion for Santa Barbara that you open like you check the weather. It answers one human question — "what's worth doing in Santa Barbara today?" — then helps you find it, save it, and share it, with the warmth and judgment of a knowing local friend.

Positioning: Find what's worth doing in Santa Barbara today — find it, save it, share it.

It's a ritual, not a search tool

You don't come to look something up — you open it each morning and it tells you what today in SB is about. The hero is the product.

It's happenings-first

The spine is what's actually happening — ranked in a three-tier cascade (dated → recurring → evergreen) so the screen is never empty. The defensible lane Yelp/Google can't own.

It's personal & frictionless

Occasion tags tune it to what you're up to, an on-device save layer needs no account, and you can share your saved list as a view-only link — no account to open it.

The north star for every design decision
Would a knowing local friend do this? They'd give you their genuine pick, not a list. They'd know it's raining and suggest indoors. They'd say "park on Anacapa, the Funk Zone lots are a trap." Every feature serves that voice — warm, specific, never breathless, never a database.

09 — The Content Model

What lives inside

Everything in SB Daymaker is a "thing" in one unified model — a place, an event, a First Look, or a happy hour — now classified by a happening tier that drives the whole-app cascade. This is what lets the hero, feed, the Near Me sort, and the saved list all draw from one pool, in one order: a tasting room's wine night and a concert are both just "happenings."

The happenings-first cascade — the spine
Every thing carries a stored happening_tier (1/2/3), a happening_category (one of 16), and a reason_to_go. Tier 1 = discrete dated happenings (this show, festival) — leads, owns the hero. Tier 2 = recurring rhythms (Wednesday wine night, First Thursday) via a recurring_schedules table. Tier 3 = evergreen, activity-framed reasons-to-go (dog beach, sunset hike) — the anti-empty floor, where no bare places are allowed. Tier is derived from structure (has-date → 1, has-schedule → 2, else 3); the AI proposes only the category. Lead-and-blend: Tier-1 leads (cap ~4), Tier-2/3 backfill to a minimum feed length, so the screen is full on quiet and busy nights.
type: place

Evergreen places — the Tier-3 floor

The standing fabric of SB: restaurants, bars, cafés, tasting rooms, shops, beaches, trails, parks. A hand-curated core of ~100–150, AI-drafted blurbs, founder-approved — each framed as a reason to go, never a bare listing. The anti-empty floor: the city is never empty.

type: firstlook

First Looks

New openings + "opening soon." Curated, high-touch, high-delight. The freshness engine that drives the weekly-check habit.

type: event

Events — Tier-1, the lead

Dated happenings now lead the cascade and own the hero when one exists. Sourced from Ticketmaster API + venue calendars + civic feeds. Ticketing hands off to AXS/TM — SB Daymaker never transacts.

type: happyhour

Happy hours — a Tier-2 layer

Operator-submitted, with the "last confirmed" freshness model. V1 ships as a curated list; the cascade unions happy_hour_windows as Tier-2 (live countdowns → Phase 2).

What every "thing" carries

The shared structure that makes the Lens, the Near Me sort, and the guides work across all types:

type (place / event / firstlook / happyhour) happening_tier (1/2/3) happening_category (1 of 16) reason_to_go title + AI editorial blurb neighborhood + nearby_zone + lat/lng price band occasion tags (+ confidence) time-of-day fit photo_source + attribution last_confirmed (freshness) date/time (Tier-1) · recurring_schedules (Tier-2)
Why unified matters
A separate "places" vs "events" model would force a merge everywhere downstream. The unified "thing" model means one pool feeds every surface — and the happening-tier classification is exactly what the happenings-first cascade requires (horizons are just time-filters over it). Adding a new content type later is a field, not a rebuild.

Plus: the Discover SB guides — neighborhood + theme

Evergreen, image-rich, single-page editorial guides — written once, refreshed occasionally. Each open guide also surfaces the live happenings scoped to it (neighborhood guides match on nearby_zone; theme guides match on tag), so a guide is never just static prose:

How content actually arrives

Ticketed shows are the easy ~10% (Ticketmaster + venue facts). The other ~90% — community happenings, new openings, grassroots and social-first events — comes from a portfolio of channels, with two public submission forms as the scaling engine:

📝 "Submit your event"

Organizers of pop-ups and grassroots happenings push their own events in (the same model the official SB tourism site uses). They can even paste an Instagram caption — AI parses it into clean fields. Incentive: the best submissions get featured in the weekend digest. This is how social-first events arrive — without scraping any platform.

🍷 "Claim / update your business"

Venues & restaurants submit happy hours, new openings, and (Phase 2) their own photos. Sourcing and business development in one form.

Supplemented by: the city civic calendar (facts), the local-media openings beat (Siteline, Edhat — monitored as leads for First Looks), human-curated monitoring of public SB accounts, and partner-authorized social feeds. Direct Facebook/Instagram scraping is deliberately out of scope — it's a terms-of-service breach that breaks constantly; SB Daymaker is a destination organizers post to, not a crawler that takes from them.

Design rules behind the set
Each tag is (1) an occasion/intent, not an identity — "what are you up to?" beats "who are you?"; (2) intelligible to locals and visitors alike; (3) mappable to real data with no per-request AI; and (4) orthogonal to the cascade — it returns things from all three tiers, and the cascade orders them. The Lens is now a strong supporting feature, not the headline differentiator (the real edge is the happenings spine + curation + ritual + local depth). One Perfect SB Day is a separate day-tripper entry point, not a tag.

12 — Delighter Moments

What makes it special

The small touches that turn a useful app into a beloved one — the things that make someone smile and come back.

🌅

The living editorial hero

The sky tracks time of day; the sun pulses softly. But now the hero also surfaces today's pick as an editorial card — not below the fold, inside the hero itself. The first thing you see tells you exactly where to be.

🌧️

It reads the weather

On a gray day the hero tag becomes "☁️ Gray day move," the pick swaps to an indoor option, and the whole secondary feed pivots to indoor-only under "The gray day playbook." An app that visibly cares about your actual day.

🆕

Being first to know

The Just Opened rail makes you the friend who knows what's new. Inherently shareable local news — "did you hear [spot] opened?" Each rail card has a heart button for a quick save without opening the detail.

🤫

Local wisdom in the copy

"Park on Anacapa, the Funk Zone lots are a trap." The blurbs carry insider texture no database has — the voice of someone who lives here.

🔗

A list you can actually send

Share your saved list and you get a real link — not just a screenshot. Whoever you're going with opens it, sees the spots, and saves their own copy to tweak. Sharing becomes social, and the link quietly grows the app.

🎯

Made-for-me feeling

Choosing "Family Day" on a clear Saturday surfaces a completely different SB than "Nightlife" on a Friday. The app feels personal without an account.

❤️

The heart pop

A spring animation plays on every save — scale up, settle back, instant confirmation. Small, but it makes saving feel satisfying rather than invisible.

🌅

One Perfect SB Day

For the ~64% of visitors here for the day, a one-tap ready-made morning-to-sunset lineup drops straight into your saved list to tweak and share. The day-tripper's front door — packaging, not new machinery.

14 — The Admin Cockpit

The founder's side

SB Daymaker is two-sided. The admin cockpit is your private, password-protected dashboard — works on desktop and mobile (approving the daily edition from your phone over coffee is the real use case). Every surface follows one pattern: the AI proposes, you approve. ~15 minutes a day keeps the whole product running.

Surface 01 — daily use

The Daily Approval queue

Opens to tomorrow's auto-drafted edition — hero pick + secondary picks + AI blurbs. Approve, or swap a pick / edit a line. Auto-publishes with your veto (ships unless you stop it). Sixty seconds.

Surface 02

The Content Review queue

Everything AI drafted that wants a glance — new blurbs, any occasion tag below the confidence threshold, and anything caught by a negative-rule gate (a Tier-1 with no date, a Tier-3 with no reason-to-go, a 21+ tagged Family Day). Violations surface loudly.

Surface 03

The Staleness board

Happy hours, recurring rhythms, and places sorted by "last confirmed," oldest first. One tap = "still good" (resets the clock) or update/remove. How time-sensitive data stays trustworthy without becoming a chore.

Surface 04

First Looks composer

Create a First Look: add the place, a photo, opening date; AI drafts the blurb in your voice; tweak, publish. The high-touch, high-delight surface.

Surface 05 — heavy at launch

Evergreen place manager

Your clean interface to seed and edit the ~150 core places — happening category, reason-to-go, neighborhood, price, occasion tags, time-of-day fit, and any recurring schedule; AI drafts the blurb; you approve.

Surface 06

Guides editor

Create and refresh the Discover SB guides (neighborhood + theme) — assemble stops, reorder, edit intros.

Why it's a co-equal MVP deliverable
The consumer app literally cannot run day-to-day without the cockpit — every AI draft, every staleness check, every First Look needs a home. It's far cheaper to build than the consumer app (Supabase gives auth + data nearly for free), and it's all one review-and-approve pattern pointed at six content types. A daily email tells you when tomorrow's edition is ready; the same cockpit assembles the 2×/week email (Wed + Fri) for your one-tap veto.

The three Tier-1 AI jobs (invisible infrastructure)

Cascade / hero ranking

Tier-1 leads and takes the hero; Tier-2/3 backfill (lead-and-blend). Weighs scarcity, marquee, weather fit, variety.

Category + occasion tagging

Proposes each thing's category (1 of 16) + occasion tags, with a confidence threshold + negative-rule safety. Tier is derived from structure, not tagged.

Editorial blurbs

Original house-voice copy from extracted facts (copyright-clean). The live app is fully deterministic — no per-tap AI.
