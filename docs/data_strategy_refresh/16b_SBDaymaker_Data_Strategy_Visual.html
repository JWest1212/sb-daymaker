The five shifts that matter

What changes, and what you get

Each shift is small on its own. Together they turn your 15-minute ceiling from a coverage limit into a quality check, and open the door to the long tail.

SHIFT 01

Sources become data, not code

Now

Sources are baked into registry.ts and regex tables. Adding one means writing code and shipping a deploy.

Recommended

Every source is a row in a sources table with its own score, schedule, and health.

Benefit: this is the keystone. Prioritization, coverage analysis, and reliability learning are all impossible until sources are rows. Add a source in seconds, not a dev session.

SHIFT 02

AI extracts events, not just adjectives

Now

AI only writes blurbs and tags for events your adapters already caught. The long tail is invisible.

Recommended

A generic AI lane reads any messy page and pulls out the event: title, date, venue, price, link.

Benefit: the winery series, the church hall show, the pop-up market, the meetup, all the events that never justified their own adapter, finally flow in. This is the real coverage jump.

SHIFT 03

A confidence score guards your time

Now

Every event lands in the review queue. More coverage means more of your minutes, so coverage is capped by your time.

Recommended

High-confidence events from trusted sources auto-publish. Only the genuinely uncertain ones reach you.

Benefit: the single biggest lever on your 15 minutes. Your daily attention becomes a spot-check on the hard cases, not a gate on everything.

SHIFT 04

Broken sources set off an alarm

Now

A source that returns nothing logs as green "0 found." You can't tell a quiet night from a dead feed.

Recommended

Each source has an expected yield. Drop below it and you get an alert, from your own verified sender.

Benefit: the cheapest defense of your "if it's happening, we have it" promise. Coverage stops rotting invisibly.

SHIFT 05

Blind spots become a weekly to-do list

Now

Gaps are invisible. Today the data is 87% dated events, a thin evergreen backstop, and whole categories near zero.

Recommended

A weekly report maps coverage by category, neighborhood, and date, and hands you cards for what's thin.

Benefit: you fix the right gaps on purpose instead of guessing, and you can measure how close you are to full coverage.

Reassurance

What does not change

None of this touches the promises the product is built on. The guardrails stay exactly where they are.

The trust rule holdsConfidence is a quality signal, never a sponsor signal. The ranker stays sponsor-blind.

Batch-only, no live AIEverything still runs in the nightly job. The app stays fully deterministic at tap time.
