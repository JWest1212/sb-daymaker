GATE 0

Stop the Bleeding

Eight fixes a visitor can catch in under two minutes. Each one directly contradicts the homepage promise that the site is "curated by someone who knows the town." No new code structure — just data and render truth.

1 · The leaked editorial note

The Funk Zone Art Walk's "Local's secret" is currently an internal note to the operator, published word for word to the public. It's the machinery showing through the exact feature meant to prove human curation.

Before
🤫 LOCAL'S SECRET
Bi-monthly (~every 8 weeks) on a FRIDAY evening, 5–8pm — NOT weekly; confirm the specific date on funkzone.net before publishing.
After
🤫 LOCAL'S SECRET
Come for the first hour: the pourers are fresh, the crowd is thin, and the food trucks on Yanonali haven't hit their line yet.

How: a nightly scan flags operator-note phrases ("before publishing," "confirm," ALL-CAPS hedges) across all published copy, so this class of leak can never reach a user again.

Blurbs were generated without reading the event's start time, so a 10 AM talk is described as an "evening" event. This appears across at least six live entries.

GATE 1

Trust Infrastructure

The load-bearing gate. It installs a publish rule so filler can't pose as curation, turns detail pages into things a visitor can actually navigate to, and propagates the guides' verification system down to every listing.

6 · Quality tiers — full cards vs. compact cards

Instead of deleting weak entries, they're demoted. A Tier 1 entry (complete, verified, with a real insider fact) renders full. A thin Tier 2 entry renders as a lighter compact row — still discoverable, but visibly not pretending to be curated. Broken Tier 3 entries are hidden from the public feed until fixed.

GATE 2

Findability

The entire organic-growth channel, currently switched off, for about a week of plumbing. Unique titles, real URLs, rich share previews, and the structured data that gets events into Google's carousel.

GATE 4

The Concierge Day

The moat. The planner goes from a filter form that hands back a list, to a constraint engine that hands back a validated day — open when it says, clustered so you're not driving in circles, parked where a local parks, and fed at mealtimes.

15 · From a filter form to a real itinerary

Today "Build my day" takes a date, an area, a daypart, and a vibe chip — that's a query, not a plan. The engine adds who's coming, a start anchor, transport, budget, and meals, then solves for a day that actually works and can be shared with one link.

GATE 5

Loops & Polish

Turns one-way browsing into loops — shareable things, shareable saved lists, a newsletter with proof, a submit page that grows the supply side — while holding the accessibility floor and leaving clean revenue seams that never touch the ranker.

16 · "Share it" finally becomes true, and Saved stops being a dead end

The tagline promises "find it, save it, share it" but there's no share anywhere, and saved items can't be shared, planned, or moved to another device. Now every thing and list shares as a designed card, a saved list becomes a plan in one tap, and the magic link moves saves phone→laptop — all with no accounts and no stored recipient info.

17 · A newsletter worth joining, a submit page that gives back

The subscribe box has no proof it's worth it, and the (genuinely smart) submit page only takes new items — never corrections, business claims, or local tips for existing spots, which is where your best future secrets live.

Held clean: device-local saves and the no-login-wall stance stay exactly as they are — a real privacy differentiator. And revenue seams (affiliate-ready links, a labeled "Featured" slot) are built but switched off, so the ranker never reads sponsor status.

What the whole build adds up to

  • Nothing contradicts itself. The leaked notes, wrong dayparts, duplicates, and broken images are gone — and can't come back, because the pipeline now guards against them.
  • Every entry earns its place. The quality gate means a full card is a promise: real address, real hours, a real insider fact, a verified date.
  • The site becomes findable. Unique titles, readable URLs, rich share cards, and event schema switch on the one free growth channel — currently at zero.
  • The depth surfaces. Search, a real "this weekend," honest recurring dates, and cross-links connect the 265 things behind the curtain.
  • The planner becomes the moat. A validated, shareable Santa Barbara day is the one thing Google, the Independent, and a generic AI cannot hand a visitor.
  • The loops close. Things, lists, and plans are all shareable artifacts; the newsletter can grow its list; the supply side grows itself — with the revenue seams clean enough to switch on later without ever touching the ranker.
