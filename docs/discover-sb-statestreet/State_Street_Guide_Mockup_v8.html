State Street and the blocks that reward wandering off it

The walk

Upper State to the old Presidio, in order. Tap a chapter to open it, mark stops ✓ Been, nine marks press the stamp.

1

Caje

1316 STATE ST · COFFEE · $  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

Start with a coffee from Caje, in the fountain courtyard across from the Arlington. Order it to go; the walk runs downhill from here.

2

The Arlington Theatre

1317 STATE ST · HISTORIC THEATER  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

The Moorish movie palace with a tiled courtyard and a ceiling painted like a night sky. Peek in even if nothing is showing.

3

Santa Barbara Public Market

38 W VICTORIA ST · FOOD HALL · $$  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

One roof, many kitchens: Thai, tacos, a wine bar, a bakery. A good place to graze or regroup before you drop into the core.

From a local

The Courthouse tower is free and rarely has a line before noon. Go up while it's early, the view is the best in town and costs you nothing.

4

The Book Den

15 E ANAPAMU ST · BOOKSTORE · $  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

California's oldest used bookstore, founded in 1902 and tucked just off the plaza on Anapamu across from the library. The stacks reward a slow browse; leave more time than you think.

5

Santa Barbara County Courthouse

1100 ANACAPA ST · LANDMARK · FREE  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

Climb the clock tower for the best free view in town, with hand-painted ceilings on the way up and the sunken gardens waiting below.

6

La Arcada Courtyard

1114 STATE ST · COURTYARD · FREE  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

The Spanish courtyard hiding off State: fountains, bronze figures tucked in corners, and a handful of small shops. The prettiest shortcut on the street.

From a local

State Street's real trick is the paseos, the tiled passages cutting between the main drag and the side streets. When the street feels busy, duck into one; you come out somewhere quieter and prettier.

7

El Presidio de Santa Barbara

123 E CANON PERDIDO · HISTORIC PARK · $  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

The 1782 Spanish fort where the city began, restored adobe by adobe. Wander the chapel and the shady courtyard, plan on under an hour. It closes at 4:30, so make this the last stop in daylight, then it is a short walk to the evening's first drink.

8

Palihouse

915 GARDEN ST · COCKTAIL BAR · $$  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

A craft cocktail in the Palihouse courtyard, a fountain, pale pink umbrellas, and market lights strung overhead. The prettiest place to catch golden hour, tucked a few steps off the beaten path in the Presidio blocks, and an easy walk on to dinner.

9

Paloma

702 ANACAPA ST · CALIFORNIO · $$$  ⌖ DIRECTIONS

Dinner on the corner of Ortega under the old neon dove, wood-fired Californio cooking in a room that has fed this block for a century. Closed Mondays and Tuesdays; the patio is the seat to want.

The take

Best hour on State Street, ranked.

1The tower climb at the Courthouse: the best free view in town, and you paid nothing for it.
2The old Presidio: the 1782 adobes and a hushed courtyard, the city at the spot where it began.
3A slow browse at the Book Den: go in for a minute, lose twenty.

Three very different hours, all a short walk apart. String them together and you have done State Street right.

THE TAKE · SB DAYMAKER

Know before you go

ParkingUse the city structures off State, not the street meters; the first 75 minutes are free and you are a block from every stop.
BudgetThe headline sights (the tower, the gardens, the Presidio, the arcade) cost little or nothing. Budget for one meal, one round, and whatever the Book Den talks you into.
RestroomsReliable restrooms at the Public Market, the Courthouse, and Paseo Nuevo; plan around those three.
TimingThis is a Wednesday to Sunday walk (Paloma is closed Mondays and Tuesdays). Start late morning: the Courthouse tower and El Presidio both close by 4:30, so see them while it is light, then the Palihouse courtyard and Paloma's dinner (from 5) carry the evening.
